The Problem

The current tourism economy in Singapore has been severely impacted by the Covid-19 pandemic, as indicated by recent studies conducted by the Singapore Tourism Board (STB), which shows a decline of 25 to 30% in visitor arrivals in 2020 (Tan et al., 2020). Against this backdrop of economic challenges, the question arises as to how to promote and strengthen Singapore's position as a leading tourist destination and what can be done to enhance the growth of Singapore's tourism industry and its constituent sectors.

Investigating The Possible Factors Affecting Tourist Arrivals search

In this section we hypothesised that the following factors may affect the tourist arrivals. We then investigated whether these factors have any correlation or similar trends with the actual tourist arrivals figures. Click on each tab to view the interactive dashboard(s).

Insights Summary

We can analysed that when there is a higher visitor rate, the higher the revenue from the tourist spendings. As such Singapore needs to grow the visitor rate to increase in tourism revenue.

Insights Summary

For the distane factor affecting the tourist arrival rate, we can infer that further the country is, the lower the visitor rates. However, we note that there are other underlying factors that is more significant (like GDP and Spending Power) that affect the visitors rates.

Insights Summary

Exchange rates affect countries differently, typically we observe that better rates leads to higher visitor rates. When countries have better exchange rates, they have more spending power. For instance, for tourists from Australia, there's a positive correlation, but for the Philippines, it's negative. One hypothesis is that Filipinos might want to travel further to other countries when exchange rates are good.

Visitor rates can also be determined by spending power, using the Big Mac index. Higher prices for a Big Mac in a visitor's home country indicate higher spending power. Price-sensitivity differs for each countries. Typically most countries will have lower visitor rates if food prices are higher in Singapore. Australia is more easily affected by food prices than the Philippines, as shown in the charts.

Insights Summary

Weather, such as the seasons and temperature, in both Singapore and the home country can also impact visitor rates. Here we map the various ranges of temperature to seasons. If the home country has hot weather, people may be less likely to visit Singapore, as they prefer to enjoy the cooler weather in their home country. This is because Singapore's hot weather all year round may also deter tourists.

Insights Summary

The number of public holidays in a given month in each country can also impact visitor rates. In general, fewer holidays in a specific month lead to higher visitor rates in Singapore, possibly because Singapore is a smaller destination with fewer activities compared to other countries.

Visitor rates are also influenced by various demographic factors. Population size and GDP have a positive correlation with visitor rates, while unemployment rates have a negative correlation. Among these factors, population size has the strongest correlation with visitor rates, as determined by correlation analysis.

Insights Summary

The most commonly searched attractions across all countries nationalities are Raffles Singapore, Orchard Road and Sentosa. People tend to visit places they research on.
However, there is a surprise finding for Orchid Garden, tourist do not research on the place but they do visit the place.

Revitalising Singapore's Tourism Economy

Strategies and Opportunities

Opportunity 1: Quarter 3 and Quarter 4 is the best time to push for sales to increase in Singapore Tourism revenue.

During July- December, it is the best time for companies who are in the Singapore tourism industry to form an alliance to push for sales, such as ensuring that the tourist attraction sites are open, and sufficient manpower to run the venue/attractions and run promotions. It would then make Singapore a more attractive destination on a whole.

Opportunity 2: Start marketing efforts to attract countries with cooler weather in their home country to visit Singapore, as from past trends they are more likely to visit Singapore.

Market Singapore to the countries that experience cooler weather as a getaway to summer.

Opportunity 3: The cultural attraction places are areas where Singapore can increase in the tourist revenue, as Singapore has yet to fully utilise the potential of these cultural attraction sites due to low number of visitors.

Singapore Tourism Board can spend efforts on marketing Singapore as a cultural place and organize activities for the tourist to boost the number of visitors. This can also be in line with Singapore's bid for UNESCO world heritage sites.
